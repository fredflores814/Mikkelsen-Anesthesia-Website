Pre-Anesthesia Instructions

Fasting Guidelines

  • No food or milk products for 8 hours before your procedure
  • No clear liquids for 2 hours before your procedure
  • Clear liquids include water, apple juice, black coffee, or tea
  • Avoid gum, mints, and candy

Medication Guidelines

  • Take blood pressure and heart medications with a small sip of water
  • Stop blood thinners 5-7 days before (as directed by your doctor)
  • Bring a list of all medications and supplements to your appointment
  • Inform us of any allergies or previous reactions to anesthesia

Transportation & Arrangements

  • Arrange for a responsible adult to drive you home
  • Someone should stay with you for 24 hours after anesthesia
  • Do not drive, operate machinery, or make important decisions for 24 hours
  • Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing

Health Preparation

  • Report any recent illnesses, especially colds or flu
  • Remove makeup, nail polish, and jewelry
  • Do not smoke for at least 12 hours before your procedure
  • Shower the night before or morning of your procedure

Post-Anesthesia Instructions

First 24 Hours

  • Rest quietly and avoid strenuous activity
  • Do not drive, drink alcohol, or take sedatives
  • Start with clear liquids and progress to light foods
  • Take prescribed medications as directed

When to Call Us

  • Fever over 101°F (38.3°C)
  • Severe nausea, vomiting, or inability to keep liquids down
  • Severe pain not controlled by prescribed medication
  • Shortness of breath or chest pain

Understanding Your Anesthesia Options

Local Anesthesia

Numbs a small area while you remain fully awake. You may feel pressure but no pain. Recovery is immediate with no lingering effects.

Moderate Sedation

You'll feel drowsy and relaxed but can respond to commands. Most patients have little to no memory of the procedure.

Deep Sedation

You're on the edge of consciousness but can still be awakened. Used for longer or more complex procedures.

General Anesthesia

You'll be completely unconscious and unaware. Commonly used for major surgical procedures or when other methods aren't suitable.

Regional Anesthesia

Numbs a larger area like an entire limb or lower body while you remain conscious. Often combined with sedation.

Monitored Care

Continuous monitoring during procedures with local anesthesia, ensuring your safety throughout the entire process.
